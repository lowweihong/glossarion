# Glossarion — Market Research Report
*Date: April 2026*

---

## Overview

Glossarion is a Graph RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) platform that allows users to upload documents and automatically expand knowledge by searching the internet, presenting results as an interactive knowledge graph. This report covers the competitive landscape, market opportunity, and target segments.

---

## The Core Concept

**Upload Document → Build Knowledge Graph → Auto-expand via Web Search → Explore Visually**

Users upload a PDF or document. The system extracts entities and relationships, builds a visual knowledge graph, then automatically searches the internet to enrich and expand that graph with relevant external information — like a "deep research agent" that starts from *your* document.

## Market Segments & Opportunity

### 1. General / Everyday Users
- Growing demand for "deep research" tools among students, professionals, and curious individuals
- ChatGPT's market share dropped from 86.7% to 64.5% in 12 months — market is fragmenting, users are shopping for better tools
- Opportunity: a friendlier, more visual alternative to Perplexity/ChatGPT for people who want to *understand* a topic deeply, not just get an answer

### 2. Legal Research — Highest Revenue Potential
- **Market size:** $3.1 billion in 2025 → projected $10.8 billion by 2030 (28.3% CAGR)
- Legal research is the **#1 revenue application** in legal AI, contributing 29% of market share
- Law firms account for **56% of all legal AI software spend**
- **Pain point:** Lawyers upload a case brief or contract and need to find related case law, regulations, and precedents across the internet — manually today, very time-consuming
- Existing tools (Westlaw, LexisNexis) are expensive and legacy; Harvey AI is enterprise-only
- **Opportunity:** An affordable tool that starts from a legal document and auto-expands to relevant case law, regulatory filings, and legal commentary

### 3. Academic Research
- Tools like Elicit and FutureHouse exist but are locked to academic paper databases
- Researchers often need to cross-reference across web sources, regulatory documents, clinical data, and press releases — not just journals
- FutureHouse launched "Falcon" for deep literature reviews — shows demand is validated
- **Opportunity:** A domain-flexible research agent that works across all source types, not just academic papers

### 4. Business Analysts & Consultants
- Use case: upload a company report or industry brief, auto-expand with competitor news, market data, regulatory changes
- Competitive research, due diligence, M&A preparation
- High willingness to pay if it saves hours of manual research

## Why Now

1. **Graph RAG is maturing** — Microsoft open-sourced GraphRAG, enterprise adoption is accelerating. The technology is proven but there is no consumer-friendly product yet.
2. **"Deep research" is a validated behavior** — OpenAI, Google, and Perplexity all launched deep research features in 2024–2025, proving users want autonomous research agents.
3. **Visual interfaces are underserved** — All existing tools output text. Nobody is giving users an interactive graph to explore their knowledge space.
4. **Legal AI is exploding** — 28% CAGR with no dominant affordable player for SMB law firms.
